Species Information.

Learn more about the amazing species featured in our gallery. Discover their habitats, behavior, diet and why they are important for our ecosystem.

üêØ Bengal Tiger

The Bengal tiger is a strong and powerful wild animal found in forests and grasslands of India and nearby countries. It has orange fur with black stripes which helps it hide while hunting. Tigers eat deer, wild boar, and other animals. Due to poaching and deforestation, their population has reduced a lot. Tigers are protected by wildlife laws and national parks.

ü¶ú Scarlet Macaw

Scarlet macaws are large, colorful tropical parrots found in the rainforests of Central and South America. They are known for their bright red, yellow, and blue feathers. These birds are very intelligent and can copy human speech. They eat fruits, nuts, and seeds, and help forests grow by spreading seeds. Due to illegal pet trade and deforestation, they are now facing serious danger.

üêç Indian Cobra

The king cobra is the longest venomous snake in the world and is found in forests of India and Southeast Asia. It feeds mainly on other snakes and small animals. Although it is dangerous, it avoids humans whenever possible. Due to deforestation and illegal capture, its population is under threat and needs protection.

üêª Brown Bear

The brown bear is a large and powerful wild animal found in forests and mountains of North America, Europe, and Asia. It has thick fur that helps it survive in cold climates. Brown bears are omnivores, meaning they eat plants, fruits, fish, insects, and small animals. They play an important role in maintaining the balance of nature. Due to habitat loss and hunting, their population is slowly decreasing

üêº Giant Panda

The giant panda is a gentle and rare animal found mainly in the bamboo forests of China. It is easily recognized by its black and white fur. Pandas spend most of their day eating bamboo and resting. Because of habitat destruction and limited food supply, their population once became very low. Today, strong conservation efforts are helping protect pandas and slowly increase their numbers.

üêò Elephant

The African elephant is the largest land animal in the world. It lives in forests and grasslands of Africa. Elephants use their long trunks to drink water, pick food, and communicate. They play an important role in maintaining forest and grassland ecosystems. Elephants are endangered because of illegal hunting for ivory and loss of habitat.

Forest Habitat

Forests are rich natural homes for a wide variety of animals such as tigers, elephants, deer, birds, monkeys, and insects. They provide essential resources like food, fresh water, shelter, and protection. Forests also help maintain the balance of nature by supporting biodiversity and regulating the climate.

Desert Habitat

Deserts are hot and dry environments where animals like camels, snakes, lizards, and foxes live. These animals are specially adapted to survive with very little water and extreme temperatures. They have strong bodies and unique behaviors that help them stay cool and conserve energy.

Snowy Mountains

Snowy mountain habitats are cold and rocky regions where animals like snow leopards, mountain goats, yaks, and polar birds survive. These animals have thick fur and strong bodies to protect them from freezing temperatures. Mountain habitats are peaceful but very challenging for survival.

Grasslands

Grasslands are open spaces covered with grasses and are home to animals like lions, zebras, deer, buffaloes, and birds. These areas provide plenty of food for herbivores and hunting grounds for predators. Grasslands support large animal populations and play an important role in the food chain.

The ocean is the largest habitat on Earth and is home to amazing creatures like whales, sharks, dolphins, fish, and turtles. It provides food, shelter, and breeding grounds for marine life. The ocean also plays a vital role in maintaining Earth‚Äôs climate and oxygen balance.

Rainforests are warm, wet, and full of life. They are home to colorful birds, monkeys, frogs, insects, snakes, and many other animals.
